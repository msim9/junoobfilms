About Us

Stories From the Margins

JUNOOB FILMS is an independent documentary platform rooted in South Punjab, Pakistan. It documents stories of communities often ignored in mainstream narratives. It is a visual archive of voices, lives, and realities.

We believe every story matters. Our work focuses on marginalized communities, culture, social issues, and the raw human experience that connects us all.

Mahjabeen Abid

Founder / Director

Mahjabeen Abid is a journalist and documentary filmmaker from Multan, South Punjab, Pakistan. Born and raised in the region she documents, her work is rooted in its land and communities. She holds a Gold Medal in Mass Communication and began her career at a regional television channel before transitioning to field reporting, where she discovered her passion for telling stories from the ground. Her reporting spans local, national, and international media platforms, both from the region and for the region, centering the voices of marginalized communities. In addition to broadcast and digital journalism, Mahjabeen has been associated with Radio Pakistan as a voiceover artist.

She directed and was cinematographer for the documentary Malangni , produced under the Patakha Pictures fellowship led by Sharmeen Obaid-Chinoy Films in collaboration with the Scottish Documentary Institute and the British Council.

Mahjabeen is a Chevening South Asia Journalism Programme Fellow (2025). During the programme, she studied at the University of Westminster in London, United Kingdom, where she engaged with journalists from across South Asia and developed a global perspective on press freedom, authoritarianism, and the role of media in promoting good governance.

Alongside international exposure and professional growth, her work remains grounded at home. Through journalism and filmmaking, she works to normalize the presence of women behind the camera in a region where it is still rare—an aspiration that led to the founding of Junoob Films.
